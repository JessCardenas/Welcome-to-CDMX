Tacos de Carnitas

Los tacos de carnitas is a flavorful and iconic Mexican dish that showcases the rich tradition of slow-cooked meats. "Carnitas", which means "little meats" in Spanish, refers to pork that has been braised or simmered in its own fat until tender, juicy, and full of flavor. The pork is typically cooked for hours, resulting in perfectly crispy edges and melt-in-your-mouth bites.

Address

Av. José Martí 142, Escandón I Secc, Miguel Hidalgo, 11800, CDMX.

Birria de Chivo

Birria is a traditional Mexican dish known for its bold, savory flavors and tender, slow-cooked meat. Originating from the state of Jalisco, this dish is typically made with either goat or beef, though other meats like lamb are sometimes used. The meat is marinated in a rich blend of chilies, herbs, and spices, then slow-cooked until it's incredibly tender and packed with flavor.

Address

Calle Salvador Díaz Mirón 114, Sta María la Ribera, Cuauhtémoc, CDMX.

This is a must

Don't forget to try the birria with a melted cheese skillet.

Chilaquiles Rellenos

This classic breakfast dish consists of crispy corn tortillas, lightly fried, then simmered in either red or green salsa until they soak up the flavor, often served with a variety of toppings to enhance their flavor and texture like cheese, cream and onions. This place does something magical with this dish, as they fill them with different toppings to make them even more delicious.

Address

Calzada Taxqueña 1500, Campestre Churubusco, 04200, CDMX.
